# Dungeon Steward Factions and Cultures

## Document Status
- Status: Working Draft
- Last Updated: 2026-04-06
- Scope: compact faction map for gameplay-facing worldbuilding
- Depends On: `design/world/canon-foundation.md`, `design/art-bible.md`

## Use of This Document
This is not a full ethnography stack. It defines the current social groups strongly implied by the game's concept, art bible, and prompt packs so they can feed cards, enemies, bosses, relics, events, and hub flavor.

## Current Faction Map

### 1. Charter-House Stewards
- Role: frontier officials, brokers, duel-capable problem-solvers, keepers of warrants, favors, seals, introductions, and practical order
- Power Base: recognized office, paperwork, social leverage, ceremonial legitimacy, and the ability to make dangerous people legible to institutions
- Material Language: velvet, satin lining, gloves, signet details, lacquer, parchment, wax, ribbon favors, brass fittings
- Public Virtues: composure, discretion, procedural intelligence, controlled charm, elegant usefulness
- Likely Blind Spots: overconfidence in formal authority, social elitism, belief that enough finesse can regularize any danger
- Gameplay Hooks:
  - cards framed as warrants, certifications, introductions, sanctioned techniques, and retained allies
  - relics framed as seals, charter tools, or office emblems
  - events framed as petitions, claims, disputes, and favors owed

### 2. Badger Wardens
- Role: lawful frontier enforcers, inspectors, gatekeepers, tunnel hardliners, and practical custodians of brute civic continuity
- Power Base: control of inspections, gates, patrols, tunnel access, and the social authority of being the ones who actually stand in the bad place
- Material Language: roughhide, iron plates, practical scarves, gloves, rope, scarred wood, soot-black and dirty-cream patterning
- Public Virtues: duty, stubbornness, credible force, procedural seriousness, refusal to be impressed
- Likely Blind Spots: rigidity, suspicion of improvisation, preference for enforceable order over subtle political nuance
- Gameplay Hooks:
  - enemy or ally lanes built around brace, hold, bulwark, gate, watch, inspection, sanction, and retaliation
  - boss or elite lanes built around over-applied law, gatekeeping, or tunnel sovereignty
  - events framed as inspections, detentions, tunnel disputes, and emergency closures

### 3. Seep-Goblin Works Crews
- Role: maintenance labor, salvage specialists, utility engineers, sluice handlers, alchemical patchers, and frontier improvisers
- Power Base: practical indispensability; they keep ugly systems functioning when elegant people would rather not touch them
- Material Language: translucent gels, bottle-glass, rusted hardware, cropped coats, waterproof satchels, straps, salvage rigs, mint-teal accents
- Public Virtues: adaptability, cleverness, repair skill, survivability, municipal practicality
- Likely Blind Spots: opportunism, informal workaround culture, low patience for ceremonial pretense, willingness to patch around root causes
- Gameplay Hooks:
  - utility/support cards, salvage relics, pressure-release events, maintenance crises, and unstable but effective tools
  - enemy lanes built around spill, patch, siphon, salvage, rupture, glue, and improvised hazard use
  - hub flavor around workshops, sluices, upkeep, and bottleneck repairs

### 4. Pard-Elf Houses
- Role: aristocratic duelist and court influence lane; prestige actors whose taste, severity, and lineage carry political weight
- Power Base: status, lineage, presentation discipline, elite training, and the ability to frame themselves as the refined standard against which others are measured
- Material Language: plum, moon-silver, lacquered leather, elegant rosette patterning, silk, cloak lining, severe posture, selective ornament
- Public Virtues: refinement, judgment, poise, controlled menace, inherited fitness for high office or precise service
- Likely Blind Spots: hauteur, aestheticized distance from maintenance realities, belief that grace is proof of legitimacy
- Gameplay Hooks:
  - duel cards, precision attacks, severe support/control effects, invitation or audience events, prestige relics, and factional rivalry bosses
  - event frames around insult, debt, invitation, prestige contest, patronage, and courtly obligation

## Relationship Matrix
| Pair | Relationship | Stable Tension |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stewards <-> Wardens | Necessary allies | finesse and paperwork versus blunt enforceability |
| Stewards <-> Goblin crews | Mutual dependence | elegant authority depends on labor it does not fully dignify |
| Stewards <-> Pard-elf houses | Prestige alliance and rivalry | office and function versus lineage and social dominance |
| Wardens <-> Goblin crews | Operational reliance | regulation and inspection versus workaround pragmatism |
| Wardens <-> Pard-elf houses | Duty versus status | hard frontier necessity versus elite claims to superior judgment |
| Goblin crews <-> Pard-elf houses | Social mistrust | indispensable maintenance versus cultivated prestige |

## Cross-Faction Shared Reality
- Every major group benefits from the frontier holding together.
- Every major group tells a story in which its own contribution is the one that really matters.
- No faction should be pure comic relief or pure villain stock.
- The dungeon's pressure forces cooperation while making status resentment worse.

## Current Worldbuilding Rule for New Groups
Any new faction or subculture should answer these questions before being treated as stable:
1. What do they maintain, extract, regulate, or protect?
2. What material bottleneck makes them relevant?
3. What virtue do they publicly claim?
4. What contradiction do they hide?
5. What enemy, boss, event, relic, or card family could naturally emerge from them?

## Expansion Candidates (not yet full factions)
- rival charter enforcers
- deserting or rogue retainers
- dungeon spillover opportunists
- threshold cults or unauthorized interpreters of dungeon signs
- mercantile interests who want the frontier profitable more than stable
