# Dungeon Steward Conflict Seeds

## Document Status
- Status: Working Draft
- Last Updated: 2026-04-06
- Scope: structural tensions that can feed enemies, bosses, relics, cards, events, and hub flavor
- Depends On: `design/world/canon-foundation.md`, `design/world/factions-and-cultures.md`, `design/world/institutions-and-locations.md`

## Use of This Document
These are not plot summaries. They are reusable pressure sources. Each seed should be able to produce at least one of:
- enemy family lane
- elite or boss lane
- card/relic naming lane
- event hook
- hub investment flavor

## Seed List

### DS-CS-01: Order vs Extraction
- Core Tension: the frontier exists to regulate danger, but it also depends on the value produced by proximity to that danger
- Stakeholders:
  - stewards want danger made governable
  - wardens want danger contained
  - opportunists want danger profitable
  - labor groups want systems funded without collapsing on them
- Why Compromise Is Hard: the same dungeon pressure that justifies authority also justifies risky expansion
- Gameplay Surfaces:
  - enemies who push too far into exploitation
  - hub upgrades with uneasy tradeoffs
  - relics tied to sanctioned extraction or containment tools

### DS-CS-02: Favor Debt vs Formal Law
- Core Tension: the frontier runs on personal ties and practical accommodations, but public order claims to run on procedure
- Stakeholders:
  - stewards need both paperwork and relationships
  - wardens distrust informal exceptions
  - elites expect graceful treatment
  - workers survive through favors and shortcuts
- Why Compromise Is Hard: the system only functions because exceptions exist, but every exception weakens legitimacy
- Gameplay Surfaces:
  - event choices about who gets certified, excused, or detained
  - cards framed as introductions, sanctions, pardons, or favors called in
  - elites or bosses built around debt, obligation, and procedural hypocrisy

### DS-CS-03: Prestige vs Maintenance
- Core Tension: visible authority belongs to elegant institutions, but actual survival depends on dirty maintenance and repair work
- Stakeholders:
  - pard-elf and steward prestige lanes want to define standards
  - goblin crews keep systems running in practice
  - wardens trust whatever holds under stress
- Why Compromise Is Hard: groups that rely on maintenance labor often under-credit it until failure becomes visible
- Gameplay Surfaces:
  - salvage or repair event hooks
  - enemies born from neglected infrastructure
  - relics that are ugly but indispensable
  - bosses or elites representing vanity over function

### DS-CS-04: Procedure vs Adaptation
- Core Tension: hard rules keep the frontier predictable, but the dungeon and frontier conditions constantly mutate faster than procedure can keep up
- Stakeholders:
  - wardens want enforceable responses
  - goblin crews improvise workarounds
  - stewards try to formalize successful exceptions
- Why Compromise Is Hard: every adaptive shortcut solves a real problem while training the system to tolerate drift
- Gameplay Surfaces:
  - defensive or utility enemy families
  - cards/relics themed around rerouting, splicing, bracing, or sanctioned exception
  - bosses representing rigid law or unstable workaround logic taken too far

### DS-CS-05: Aristocratic Legitimacy vs Frontier Necessity
- Core Tension: prestige cultures claim they know what proper order looks like, but frontier survival often favors the merely competent over the beautiful or well-born
- Stakeholders:
  - pard-elf houses and elite lanes defend inherited standards
  - stewards must work with whoever is useful
  - wardens and labor groups judge legitimacy by performance under pressure
- Why Compromise Is Hard: prestige loses force if competence becomes the only accepted measure, but competence alone does not settle who gets to rule or represent the frontier
- Gameplay Surfaces:
  - duelists, inspectors, elite retainers, invitation events, honor or insult encounters
  - bosses built around ceremony, jurisdiction, or aestheticized force

### DS-CS-06: Civic Continuity vs Dungeon Spill
- Core Tension: the frontier cannot abandon the threshold, but every contact with the dungeon risks contamination, mutation, breach, or political panic
- Stakeholders:
  - charter-house wants continuity
  - wardens want hard containment
  - opportunists push for deeper reach
  - everyone fears visible failure
- Why Compromise Is Hard: the system must interact with danger to justify itself, but interaction steadily changes the system
- Gameplay Surfaces:
  - threshold monsters or warped officials
  - relics tied to quarantine, seals, bells, or breach tools
  - map/event hooks around closure, rerouting, or partial collapse

### DS-CS-07: Public Service vs Private Advantage
- Core Tension: offices are framed as duties, but every office also creates chances for private leverage, patronage, and selective generosity
- Stakeholders:
  - officials, retainers, petitioners, labor leads, elite patrons
- Why Compromise Is Hard: a frontier with no flexibility becomes cruel, but a frontier with too much selective favor becomes corrupt and brittle
- Gameplay Surfaces:
  - reward choices with social cost framing
  - relics tied to office perks, signed favors, or special access
  - enemy families based on sanctioned abuse, rival claims, or grudge enforcement

## Enemy Family Lanes
- rival charter enforcers
- seep-corrupted tunnel raiders
- deserter retainers and failed sworn blades
- overzealous inspection crews
- threshold scavengers who turned private advantage into open predation

## Boss Lanes
- legal/civic monstrosity
- moonlit predator aristocrat
- charm-corrupted frontier authority
- monumental tunnel sovereign

## Event Hook Lanes
- disputed warrant or seal
- inspection gone wrong
- maintenance bottleneck or sluice failure
- invitation with hidden obligation
- gate closure, quarantine, or disputed jurisdiction
- salvage claim versus public safety

## Relic and Card Flavor Feed
Reusable objects and motifs that naturally emerge from the seeds above:
- warrants
- seals
- bells
- medallions
- keys
- gate tools
- inspection badges
- stitched repairs
- lacquered favors
- sanctioned workaround devices

## Escalation Rule
Prefer escalation that feels like world pressure becoming embodied:
- local inconvenience -> institutional dispute -> visible breach -> elite/mini-boss -> boss or threshold crisis
