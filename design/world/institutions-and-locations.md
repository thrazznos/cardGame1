## Document Status

- Last Updated: 2026-04-06

## Why This Document Exists
Dungeon Steward's world already implies strong spaces: charter-house halls, parlors, works, gates, tunnels, and thresholds. This document turns those spaces into usable setting structures instead of moodboard fragments.

## Core Institution: The Charter-House
- Working Definition: a frontier civic outpost that combines office, residence, logistics, reception, and regulated dungeon-adjacent administration
- Function: process claims, hold authority symbols, manage local order, host negotiations, broker favors, and keep the frontier's dangerous business legible
- Associated Factions: stewards first, but also wardens, goblin crews, petitioners, and visiting elites
- Visual / Material Lane: warm lamplight, cool stone, parchment, wax, brass, lacquered wood, controlled velvet ornament, practical storage, and visible procedural clutter
- Gameplay Use:
  - hub anchor
  - investment fantasy source
  - reward framing and petitions
  - future event staging ground

## Key Location Lanes

### 1. Charter-House Halls
- Function: circulation, reception, visible authority, public-facing movement
- Mood: orderly but busy, ceremonial but lived-in
- Best Uses: hub overviews, NPC traffic, public notices, oath or warrant presentation, future event scenes

### 2. Parlors / Salon Decision Rooms
- Function: negotiation, audience, prestige encounters, controlled favors, delicate decisions
- Mood: soft light, measured ornament, social danger instead of physical danger
- Best Uses: elite events, invitation scenes, relic/favor framing, rare reward presentation, future story choices

### 3. Frontier Offices and Stores
- Function: records, supplies, triage paperwork, procedural life, visible scarcity management
- Mood: parchment, ledgers, stacked cases, keys, seals, practical clutter under attempted elegance
- Best Uses: hub investments, logistics-flavored events, card/relic naming inspiration, bureaucratic humor that stays in-world

### 4. Goblin Works and Sluices
- Function: maintenance, repair, pressure release, salvage sorting, unstable utility labor
- Mood: damp, clever, patchwork, glass-and-metal practicality, barely controlled systems
- Best Uses: support/utility flavor, salvage relics, repair events, unstable enemy or hazard motifs

### 5. Warden Gates and Inspection Posts
- Function: checkpoint control, tunnel access, quarantine, security, hard enforcement
- Mood: iron, roughhide, gates, lantern discipline, lists, sanction boards, practical force
- Best Uses: elite fights, procedural conflict, threshold scenes, boss telegraph lane, map node identity

### 6. Tunnels and Watch Routes
- Function: the practical edge where governance gives way to danger
- Mood: compressed space, stone pressure, inspection marks, barricades, old repairs, audible threat
- Best Uses: encounter setup, enemy family logic, hazard flavor, map path identity

### 7. The Dungeon Threshold
- Function: the line where chartered order encounters whatever the dungeon really is
- Mood: dangerous adjacency, ceremonial caution, necessary incursion, destabilized certainty
- Best Uses: boss framing, threshold events, map transitions, high-stakes relic or warrant flavor

## Institution Rules for Future Content
1. Every major location should imply who maintains it.
2. Every major institution should imply what resource, danger, or bottleneck justified its creation.
3. A location is stronger if it can support at least one of these: combat lane, event lane, relic lane, naming lane, or hub investment lane.
4. Courtly ornament must never erase the sense that people actually work here.

## Practical Questions This Doc Helps Answer

- What kind of room or checkpoint would produce a particular enemy or elite encounter?
- What does the frontier look like when it is trying to be orderly under pressure?
