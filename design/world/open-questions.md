# Dungeon Steward World Open Questions

## Document Status
- Status: Open Question
- Last Updated: 2026-04-06
- Scope: unresolved world issues that should not silently harden into canon

## Use of This Document
This file is the parking lot for real questions, not vague wishes. If something is unsettled, it should live here until explicitly resolved in another world doc.

## Current Questions

### OQ-01: What exactly is the charter-house?
- Why it matters: it defines the hub fantasy, steward authority, reward framing, and investment flavor
- Current options in play:
  - civic outpost of a larger frontier administration
  - licensed semi-private order with public obligations
  - hybrid administrative-commercial house

### OQ-02: What does the dungeon produce besides combat danger?
- Why it matters: determines economy, hub upgrades, enemy motivations, and relic flavor
- Current unknowns:
  - salvage value
  - magical spill or contamination
  - prestige resources
  - legal claims tied to threshold access

### OQ-03: How formal are charms, favors, and markers?
- Why it matters: affects card framing, relics, social stakes, and the line between ornament and institution
- Current unknowns:
  - court custom only
  - legal token system
  - semi-magical affiliation sign
  - all of the above in overlapping contexts

### OQ-04: What is the exact political role of pard-elf houses?
- Why it matters: shapes prestige conflict, event framing, and enemy/boss direction
- Current unknowns:
  - local governing elite
  - outside houses with frontier stakes
  - specialist duel/judgment caste embedded in charter life

### OQ-05: Which non-charter actors contest frontier legitimacy?
- Why it matters: future enemy families and event lines need opposition groups that are not just monsters
- Current candidates:
  - rival charter enforcers
  - independent extractors or profiteers
  - threshold cults
  - deserters or disavowed retainers

### OQ-06: What makes a boss a boss in-world?
- Why it matters: determines whether bosses are mostly monsters, political authorities, corrupted officials, or threshold sovereigns
- Current candidates:
  - concentrated office
  - institutional failure made flesh
  - dungeon spill at scale
  - aristocratic or civic figure pushed into singular dominance

### OQ-07: How much of the frontier is actually governed versus merely claimed?
- Why it matters: affects map flavor, hub ambition, and the gap between paperwork and reality

## Resolution Rule
When a question is answered:
1. move the answer into the correct world doc
2. remove or rewrite the open question
3. if the answer is durable, sync the distilled fact with `honcho-world-sync`
